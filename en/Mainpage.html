About the series Berserk

Berserk is a long running, widely popular manga series, by the manga author Kentaro Miura.
The series is split into arcs such as:

  • The introductory arc - Black Swordsman
  • Retrospective arc, giving more context to the world and its characters - the Golden Age
  • Continuation of the story, from where Black Swordsman ended - Conviction arc
  • A more parallel story driven arc, switching from one side to the other post-Conviction - Falcon of the Millenium Empire
  • And at the time of writing this list, the current arc - Fantasia

The series bolsters a great number of both main and minor characters, which may or may not appear for more than one arc!
Berserk's story is a dark, medieval fantasy, in both its setting and themes.

Context for the Eclipse

Eclipse is an event that is very crucial to the story of Berserk and its characters. It's the culmination of the story of Golden Age, resulting in betrayal, one's dream taking higher priority than the lives of others, to ascend to this twisted image of Godhood.

 
This person is none other than the tortured and mutilated Griffith, knowing his life long goal, his dream of ruling a kingdom, will not come to fruition.
That is, until he happens to stumble upon his old keepsake, an amulet called the Behelit. What seemed otherwise as nothing else than a simple, absurd looking amulet, is a mark. A mark of a person, destined to rise to Godhood.

 
This is where the event of the Eclipse comes into play. It isn't some astrological phenomenon like the name might suggest, but a supernatural event, that occurs once every 216 years. For in the world of Berserk, lies the rule of Causality. Causality is mostly like the concept of destiny, with a twist.

 
Something is meant to happen, what isn't determined however, is HOW the events play out up to that point. Griffith was chosen to become such a God, a part of the group called the Godhand. But Godhood comes at a great price. Sacrificing those most dear to you, simple as that. At that point, Griffith ceased to be and in his place the being Femto has been born.

 
And so the demon feast had begun, the entirety of the Band of the Hawk, marked. Marked by the Mark of Sacrifice. No one present survives this event, once marked. All with the exception of two people.. Guts and Casca. Femto had kept a twisted sliver of his former being and exacted petty revenge, by raping his once loyal right hand, Casca and making her now lover, Guts witness this heinous act, as an act of petty revenge. Guts ended up cutting off his own left arm and losing his right eye to a demon, aswell as Casca losing her self.
 
Guts and Casca survived this heinous event only thanks to the aid of a mysterious, seemingly superhuman rider, Skull Knight, whom Guts had ran into before
